# 📘 IB Psychology (First Assessment 2025)

## Paper 1 – Structure, Requirements & Question Framework

---

## 1. Overview of Paper 1

**Duration:** 1 hour 30 minutes
**Total Marks:** 35

**Weighting:**

* **Standard Level (SL):** 35%
* **Higher Level (HL):** 25%

Paper 1 assesses students’ ability to:

* Demonstrate knowledge of core psychological approaches
* Apply psychological understanding to contextualised situations
* Engage with key concepts in a structured and analytical way
* Construct clear, focused written responses under timed conditions

Paper 1 is common to both SL and HL students.

---

## 2. Core Content Assessed

### A. Psychological Approaches

Questions draw from the three core approaches:

* **Biological approach**
* **Cognitive approach**
* **Sociocultural approach**

Students are expected to understand:

* Theories and models
* Core terminology
* Relevant applications
* Methodological considerations where appropriate

---

### B. Key Concepts

Students must demonstrate engagement with at least one of the six key concepts:

* Bias
* Causality
* Change
* Measurement
* Perspective
* Responsibility

Conceptual understanding is particularly important in Section C.

---

### C. Contexts

Questions may be situated within one of four contexts:

* Health and well-being
* Human development
* Human relationships
* Learning and cognition

Sections B and C are explicitly contextualised.

---

# 3. Paper 1 Structure

Paper 1 consists of **three sections: A, B, and C.**

---

# Section A – Short-Answer Questions

### Marks: 4 + 4

### Total: 8 marks

### Requirement: Answer both questions

---

## Purpose

Section A assesses:

* Foundational knowledge
* Conceptual clarity
* Ability to explain psychological content concisely

---

## Question Type

* Short-answer format
* Focused on theory or concept explanation
* Drawn from two different psychological approaches
* Context is not explicitly specified

---

## Expected Response Features

High-quality responses should:

* Clearly define key terminology
* Explain the theory or concept accurately
* Include a relevant example
* Use appropriate psychological vocabulary
* Remain concise and structured

**Length Guidance:** Approximately 8–10 focused sentences per response.

---

## Skill Emphasis

* Knowledge and understanding
* Basic application

---

# Section B – Application Questions

### Marks: 6 + 6

### Total: 12 marks

### Requirement: Answer both questions

---

## Purpose

Section B assesses the ability to:

* Apply psychological knowledge to unseen scenarios
* Demonstrate analytical reasoning
* Connect theory directly to contextual material

---

## Question Type

* Scenario-based short answers
* Each question linked to one of the four contexts
* Requires explanation of how theory applies to the situation provided

Students must go beyond describing theory and explicitly apply it to details in the scenario.

---

## Expected Response Features

High-quality responses should:

* Identify relevant theory or model
* Explain the theory accurately
* Refer directly to scenario details
* Demonstrate logical reasoning
* Show explicit “link-back” statements

**Length Guidance:** Approximately 12–14 well-developed sentences.

---

## Skill Emphasis

* Application
* Analysis
* Structured reasoning

---

# Section C – Extended Response

### Marks: 15

### Requirement: Answer one question (choice of two)

---

## Purpose

Section C evaluates:

* Depth of understanding
* Conceptual integration
* Critical thinking
* Evaluative reasoning
* Argument construction

---

## Question Type

* Concept-based extended response
* Two questions provided
* Each question linked to a different context
* Students choose one

Command terms typically require higher-order thinking (e.g., evaluate, discuss).

---

## Expected Response Features

High-quality essays should:

* Address the command term directly
* Integrate at least one key concept meaningfully
* Demonstrate clear theoretical understanding
* Present balanced critical analysis
* Include strengths, limitations, and implications
* Develop a coherent argument
* End with a reasoned conclusion

---

## Recommended Essay Structure

1. Introduction

   * Define key terms
   * Establish focus
   * Outline argument

2. Theoretical Explanation

   * Clear, structured explanation of relevant theory

3. Analytical Development

   * Apply concept
   * Compare perspectives if relevant

4. Evaluation

   * Strengths
   * Limitations
   * Methodological or conceptual considerations

5. Conclusion

   * Clear, justified judgement

**Length Guidance:** 3–4 well-developed paragraphs (approximately 600–800 words).

# 5. Comparative Skill Weighting

Paper 1 progressively increases in cognitive demand:

* **Section A:** Knowledge clarity
* **Section B:** Applied reasoning
* **Section C:** Synthesis and evaluation

The examination is designed to assess both accuracy and depth of thinking.

# 6. Preparation Implications for Schools

### Section A Preparation

* Weekly 4-mark drills
* Terminology precision exercises
* Concise writing training

### Section B Preparation

* Scenario workshops
* Explicit application modelling
* Context rotation practice

### Section C Preparation

* Concept mapping
* Timed essay practice
* Evaluation paragraph drills
* Command term deconstruction
