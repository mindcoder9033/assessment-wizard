# Paper 3 – Resource Booklet (HL)

**Subject:** Psychology
**Assessment Focus:** Data analysis and interpretation
**Linked Examination:** Paper 3 (HL)
**Total Marks (Exam Paper):** 30
**Duration:** 1 hour 45 minutes

This resource booklet accompanies the Paper 3 examination paper and contains the research sources required to answer all questions.

---

# 1. Cover Page – Instructions to Candidates

## Instructions

* This booklet contains the research sources required for Paper 3.
* Read all sources carefully before answering the examination questions.
* All responses must refer directly to the relevant source(s).
* Use appropriate psychological terminology throughout.
* Where required, integrate your HL extension knowledge with the information provided.
* Do not write answers in this booklet unless instructed to do so.
* The sources may include quantitative and qualitative data.

## Important Notes

* All four questions in Paper 3 rely on this booklet.
* Some questions focus solely on data interpretation.
* Other questions require synthesis of multiple sources.
* The claim for the extended response question will appear in the examination paper, not in this booklet.

---

# 2. Source Structure

The resource booklet includes multiple research sources related to **one HL extension inquiry topic**.

## HL Extension Inquiry Topic

All sources will relate to one of the following topics:

* The role of culture in shaping behavior
* The role of motivation in shaping behavior
* The role of technology in shaping behavior

The specific topic assessed will be indicated implicitly through the sources.

---

## Types of Sources Included

The booklet may contain:

### Quantitative Sources

* Tables of numerical data
* Statistical results
* Graphs and charts
* Experimental findings
* Correlational findings

Quantitative sources may be drawn from:

* Experimental research
* Non-experimental research

---

### Qualitative Sources

* Interview excerpts
* Observational summaries
* Thematic analyses
* Researcher interpretations

Qualitative sources may include:

* Descriptions of research context
* Methodological explanations
* Participant quotations

---

## Nature of the Data

* Data may be authentic research findings or constructed specifically for assessment purposes.
* Studies may be experimental or non-experimental.
* Each source will contain sufficient information to allow:

  * Interpretation of findings
  * Evaluation of research considerations
  * Synthesis across sources

---

## Number and Organization of Sources

* The booklet typically contains multiple labeled sources (e.g., Source 1, Source 2, Source 3, etc.).
* Sources may vary in length and complexity.
* Some sources will primarily support Questions 1–3.
* At least three sources must be used for the extended response question (Question 4).

---

# How the Resource Booklet Supports Assessment

The sources are designed to assess:

* Interpretation of graphical and numerical data
* Analysis of research findings
* Evaluation of credibility, bias, or transferability
* Integration of multiple perspectives
* Application of HL extension knowledge to unseen material

Students are expected to:

* Refer explicitly to source material
* Interpret data accurately
* Identify methodological strengths and limitations
* Compare and synthesize findings across sources
* Develop reasoned conclusions supported by evidence

---

# Preparation Guidance

To use the resource booklet effectively, students should:

* Practise interpreting graphs and statistical summaries
* Develop understanding of qualitative research criteria
* Be familiar with research design terminology
* Understand challenges in psychological research
* Develop structured analytical writing
