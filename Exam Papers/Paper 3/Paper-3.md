# Paper 3 – External Assessment Guide (HL)

**Subject:** Psychology
**Assessment Focus:** Data analysis and interpretation of research data
**Total Marks:** 30
**Duration:** 1 hour 45 minutes
**Weighting:** HL only – 30%

**Assessment Objectives Assessed:**

* **AO2:** Application and analysis
* **AO3:** Synthesis and evaluation

Paper 3 assesses students’ ability to interpret research data, analyze findings, evaluate research considerations, and synthesize information from multiple sources within one Higher Level (HL) extension.

---

# 1. Cover Page Information

## Instructions to Candidates

* Answer **all four questions**.
* All questions are based on the **provided resource booklet**.
* Use relevant psychological terminology throughout.
* Support responses with:

  * Direct reference to the sources
  * Your knowledge of the HL extension
* Show clear reasoning when interpreting data.
* Structure extended responses logically and coherently.

---

# Nature of the Sources

* Sources may include **quantitative and qualitative data**.
* Research may be **experimental or non-experimental**.
* Data may be authentic or created for assessment purposes.
* Sources will relate to **one HL extension inquiry topic**, such as:

  * The role of culture in shaping behavior
  * The role of motivation in shaping behavior
  * The role of technology in shaping behavior

The focus of assessment includes:

* Interpretation of graphs and data
* Analysis of findings
* Evaluation of research considerations
* Synthesis across multiple sources

# 2. Section A – Interpretation and Analysis (9 Marks)

Section A assesses data interpretation, analysis, and research understanding.

---

## Question 1 – Interpretation of Graphs (3 Marks)

### Question Type

* Short response
* Focus on identifying and explaining a limitation in data interpretation

### Requirement

Students must:

* Identify one issue that limits interpretation of the data in a given source
* Clearly explain why this issue restricts interpretation

### Skills Assessed

* Understanding of data presentation
* Critical evaluation of graphical information
* Clarity of explanation

### Marking Overview

| Marks | Performance Description          |
| ----- | -------------------------------- |
| 1     | Relevant issue identified        |
| 2     | Relevant issue clearly described |
| 3     | Relevant issue fully explained   |

---

## Question 2 – Data Analysis (6 Marks)

### Question Type

* Short analytical response

### Requirement

Students must:

* Analyze findings from one provided source
* State a clear conclusion
* Explicitly link the conclusion to the data

### Expectations

* Accurate interpretation of results
* Logical reasoning
* Clear connection between findings and conclusion

### Marking Overview

| Marks | Performance Description                                   |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1–2   | Limited or inaccurate analysis; unclear conclusion        |
| 3–4   | Accurate analysis but lacks development; basic conclusion |
| 5–6   | Detailed analysis; clear and explicitly linked conclusion |

---

# 3. Section B – Research Considerations (6 Marks)

## Question 3 – Qualitative Research Considerations

### Question Type

* Focused evaluative discussion

### Possible Focus Areas

Students may be asked to discuss:

* How credibility could be improved
* How bias could be avoided
* The extent to which findings are transferable

### Requirement

Students must:

* Demonstrate understanding of the relevant research consideration
* Apply it directly to the provided source
* Support discussion with explicit reference to evidence from the source

### Skills Assessed

* Understanding of qualitative methodology
* Evaluation of research quality
* Application to unseen material

### Marking Overview

| Marks | Performance Description                              |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| 1–2   | Limited understanding; little reference to source    |
| 3–4   | Some understanding; implicit reference to source     |
| 5–6   | Detailed understanding; explicit reference to source |

---

# 4. Section C – Synthesis and Evaluation (15 Marks)

## Question 4 – Extended Response

### Question Type

* Extended analytical essay
* Based on multiple sources

### Requirement

Students must:

* Use **at least three sources**
* Apply relevant HL extension knowledge
* Discuss the validity of a stated claim
* Consider different perspectives
* Reach a reasoned conclusion

### Expectations of a High-Quality Response

A strong response typically includes:

### Introduction

* Clear understanding of the claim
* Identification of relevant sources

### Analysis of Sources

* Interpretation of findings
* Integration of knowledge from the HL extension
* Clear linkage to the claim

### Evaluation

* Consideration of strengths and limitations
* Discussion of validity
* Consideration of alternative viewpoints

### Conclusion

* Clearly reasoned judgment
* Consistent with arguments presented
